# 🛡️ Day 01 – Cybersecurity Fundamentals

## 📖 What is Cybersecurity?

Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting computers, networks, systems, applications, and data from cyber threats such as hackers, malware, phishing attacks, and unauthorized access.

The primary goal of cybersecurity is to ensure that digital information remains secure, private, and available to authorized users.

---

# 🎯 Why is Cybersecurity Important?

Cybersecurity helps to:

- Protect personal and business information.
- Prevent cyber attacks.
- Maintain privacy.
- Secure financial transactions.
- Protect critical infrastructure.
- Build trust in digital systems.

---

# 🔺 The CIA Triad

The CIA Triad is the foundation of cybersecurity.

## 1. Confidentiality

Confidentiality means that information should only be accessible to authorized people.

### Example

Your email password should only be known by you.

---

## 2. Integrity

Integrity ensures that information remains accurate and is not modified without permission.

### Example

A student's exam result should not be changed by an unauthorized person.

---

## 3. Availability

Availability means that systems and data are accessible whenever authorized users need them.

### Example

An online banking website should be available 24/7.

---

# ⚠️ Common Cyber Threats

## Malware

Malicious software designed to damage or steal data.

Examples include:

- Virus
- Worm
- Trojan Horse
- Ransomware
- Spyware

---

## Phishing

Fake emails or websites designed to steal usernames, passwords, or financial information.

---

## Social Engineering

Manipulating people into revealing confidential information.

---

## Password Attacks

Attempts to guess or steal passwords.

Examples:

- Brute Force Attack
- Dictionary Attack

---

## Denial of Service (DoS)

An attack that overwhelms a server or network so legitimate users cannot access it.

# 📚 Key Terms

Cybersecurity – Protecting digital systems.

Threat – Anything that can cause harm.

Vulnerability – A weakness in a system.

Risk – The possibility that a threat exploits a vulnerability.

Attack – An attempt to compromise a system.

Defense – Measures taken to protect systems.
