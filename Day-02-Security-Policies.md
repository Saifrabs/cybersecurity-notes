Day 02 - Security Policies

What is a Security Policy?

A Security Policy is a set of rules and guidelines that helps protect an organization's information, systems, and networks from security threats. It tells users what they are allowed to do and what they are not allowed to do.

---

Why are Security Policies Important?

- Protect sensitive information
- Reduce cybersecurity risks
- Ensure employees follow security rules
- Meet legal and regulatory requirements
- Improve business continuity

---

Types of Security Policies

1. Acceptable Use Policy (AUP)

Defines how employees should properly use company computers, internet, and other IT resources.

2. Password Policy

Specifies password requirements such as:

- Minimum length
- Complexity
- Password expiration
- Password reuse restrictions

3. Access Control Policy

Determines who can access specific systems, applications, and data.

4. Incident Response Policy

Provides steps to follow when a security incident or cyberattack occurs.

5. Backup and Recovery Policy

Explains how important data is backed up and restored after data loss or system failure.

---

Best Practices

- Create strong passwords.
- Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA).
- Keep software updated.
- Never share passwords.
- Report suspicious activities immediately.
- Follow company security guidelines.

---

Key Takeaways

- Security policies protect people, systems, and data.
- Every organization should have clear cybersecurity policies.
- Employees play an important role in maintaining security.
